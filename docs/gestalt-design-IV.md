# Gestalt-theoretic design, a fourth reading — concrete thinking and the number-Gestalt

> **What this is.** A *fourth* design essay, written after a direct encounter with the
> **earliest** of Wertheimer's major works — *Über das Denken der Naturvölker: Zahlen
> und Zahlgebilde* ("On the Thinking of the Naturvölker: Numbers and Number-Gestalts,"
> 1912), received for publication in October 1911. The first three essays
> ([`gestalt-design.md`](gestalt-design.md), [`-II`](gestalt-design-II.md),
> [`-III`](gestalt-design-III.md)) quarried five *later* texts (1934–1959) for
> part/piece, structural truth (tF/fT), the Beethoven test, productive-thinking
> gap→vector, recentering (*Umzentrierung*), and **topological** centering (the radix
> read off the dependency DAG). This essay does not restate any of that. It mines the
> 1912 text for what the later ones do not carry: a positive theory of **concrete
> thinking** and of the **non-uniform, situated, Gestalt-structured magnitude-series**
> — and it turns that theory on the part of this tool the later essays left untouched:
> its *numbers*. When essay and code disagree, trust the code.
>
> **Provenance.** Read directly for this essay: Wertheimer, *Numbers and
> Number-Gestalts* (1912), §§1–20 in full. The 1934 (*On Truth*) and 1937
> (*Democracy*) sources are cross-referenced from essays I–III where the doctrines
> meet (tF/fT, *Umzentrierung*), not re-derived here. A word on the source's frame:
> it is an early-twentieth-century ethnography, and its organizing term *Naturvölker*
> ("nature-peoples") is of its period and not ours. What is alive in it — and what is
> taken here — is the **cognitive thesis**, not the anthropology: a phenomenology of
> two modes of mind, the abstract-uniform and the concrete-situated. That thesis turns
> out to bear directly on a tool built for one concrete mind under load.
>
> **A note on standing.** This is a *proposal* layer, like essay II when it was
> written. It names exact code sites and gives the contributions even weight, but
> **changes no code.** What ships, and when, is recorded in `STATUS.md` →
> "Gestalt roadmap" and the migration-log, which are canonical. Read the contributions
> below for the *why*; trust the code for the *what*.

## I. The fresh encounter — concrete thinking and the number-Gestalt

The later texts are about *thinking*; this one is about *number*, and is therefore,
unexpectedly, the most useful of the six for a tool whose central act is to count and
size and divide a manuscript. Its argument runs against a prejudice it names in its
first pages: that other peoples' number-sense is "nothing but a preliminary
stage — merely vaguer, more rudimentary, and perhaps less capable" of *our* number.
Wertheimer's counter-question is the one worth importing: not *which of our numbers do
they have?* but *what kinds of thought-configurations [Denkgebilde] do they possess in
this domain, and what cognitive tasks do these serve?*

Six threads of that answer are gold the later essays never touched.

**1. Number-Gestalt vs. abstract number.** Our number is abstract: transferable across
any material, built of uniform steps, with every operation applicable to every number
identically (Locke's "and one more, and one more"). Against this Wertheimer sets the
*Zahlgebilde*, the number-**configuration**: "five" as the hand, the quincunx, the
five-form grasped at once as a whole-shape. When trunks are fetched for a house, "one
has a group-configuration of the posts that presents itself quite concretely in the
*Gestalt* of the house." These configurations are "richer in content and of more
limited transferability than abstract numbers." They are *situated* — *beheimatet*,
at home in their material: you can count money and tree-trunks but, in some places,
not villages; "four rifles, four bows, four warriors" make a four, but "four grains
of rice are not 'four' in the same sense; they are likely equivalent to nothing." A
rider and a horse do not make two.

**2. Summarizing by Gestalt vs. abstracting by feature.** The generalization that
holds a manifold together is *not* the and-sum of differentiae. A "rectangle" is not
"four-sided + right-angled" arrived at by subtraction; it is a *typus* that holds
elongated, broad, narrow, upright, lying — "the common *Gestalt*-quality of the
typical rectangular *Gestalten*." Proof: "a shape 2 m long and 1 cm wide will be
recognized as a rectangle only with astonishment — or, more precisely, will be newly
acquired as one of the possibilities of representation." The general concept-analogue
is therefore *richer* than the species, not poorer. And — the line that should be
engraved over this whole project — the concept-analogues that are "primary and vital"
in practical life and in art "function not by abstracting according to features, but
by **summarizing according to *Gestalt***." That is, verbatim and from 1912, the
tool's own founding summary-vs-exegesis thesis.

**3. The non-uniform, situated series.** The natural "number series" does not proceed
in equal steps *ex principio structurae*. It is a graded mixture: individual
group-Gestalts at the low end; then **approximate Gestalts with zones of indifference**
("a dozen" is often not exactly twelve but "a more specific stepped-configuration of
'many'"; "a man in his thirties"); then several distinct kinds of "much / many"; then,
at the top, "simply many" — "more than it is possible or meaningful to specify."
Studded through it are **privileged numbers** (round numbers; the hand as five; the
*nádavek*, the baker's extra loaf, so that "in the domain of the dozen there is really
no '13'; there is only a dozen and a bonus"). Crucially, exactness is not a free good:
"There are tasks for which natural thinking intends numerical exactness only up to the
limits given by the nature of the task itself." Past that limit precision is not
merely wasted but *wrong*: carry a calculation "beyond a certain decimal place [and]
the further decimal places are false and meaningless."

**4. Quasi-local predetermination — a second sense of centering.** Essay III gave
*topological* centering: the radix as "the source of the arrows." §17 gives a different
one. A number has a **place within the extent of a whole**: 101 is "near 100, the next
one"; 50 is "the middle of 100"; 7 sits in ten as (7 + 3). The whole is given first,
with its predetermined divisions, and the part is *localized* within it. There is even
a circular form — the clock, where "11 + 5 = 4" is not nonsense but a position read off
a cyclic whole, "a chain of unique, always individual elements, a chain that cannot be
started at an arbitrary point." And there is a structural reading of the same point:
*a × b ≠ b × a*, because "taking 5 plants ('one hand' of plants) 3 times is, in its
result, something different from taking 3 plants 5 times" — the multiplier *structures*,
the multiplicand is *quantity*. From which: "one of the most important skills in
calculation is being able to see smaller groups within a larger heap or group."

**5. Division by natural articulation.** This is the richest vein for a tool that cuts
a manuscript into parts. "What is generally decisive for division is not the equality
of the parts. Rather, it is … a predetermination inherent in the *Gestalt* of the
whole … and the tendency to obtain, as a result of the division, new natural, unitary
wholes." "It is a psychological fiction that any arbitrary division is, in all cases,
equally valid and merely a matter of choice." Divide a spear and you get a spearhead
and a shaft, not "two." And the images that decide it: "half a pot is not a pot, but a
shard"; "**Parts must be possible as meaningful parts of the whole**." A division that
yields shards — pieces that are not themselves wholes — has failed, however equal the
pieces.

**6. Number ≠ value/efficacy.** "Number and value, or number and efficacy, are by no
means always parallel." There are diminishing returns ("each successive, equal addition
corresponds to a lawfully diminishing increase in value"); minimum thresholds (a single
grain, a single drop, does nothing); and outright inversion — "less would be more; …
the half is more than the whole." "One cannot buy a house with a mountain of copper
small change." "1 + 100 means something different from 100 + 1." Count is a proxy for
worth, and a treacherous one.

Behind all six stands the §20 résumé and its method. The central question is "what
kinds of structures, operations, and abilities are present in the domain of the
categorical 'how much'" — given that these structures "are not centered in the same way
as our mathematics," which "emphasizes the equality of quantities and abstracts from
the … value given to a number by its material context." And a methodological warning we
will need: it is "completely inadvisable to constantly demand that people do something
that seems absurd, counter-intuitive, or senseless to them," or you reap "responses
born of caprice, boredom, or vexation … fatigue and 'headaches.'" Best to give "not …
any abstract tasks at all, but rather 'embedded' tasks that are genuinely meaningful."

These six are siblings. Each is the same move — *from an abstract-uniform encoding to a
concrete-situated one* — made in a different register. Concrete thinking is the soil;
the contributions below grow from it side by side.

## II. Where the app stands

Read against this text, the tool is already, in large measure, a concrete-thinking
instrument — and was before this essay. The whole/part model is genuine: a manuscript
is a recursive `Section` tree, and the synthetic root (`buildRootSection`) lets every
operation address the *whole* and not merely a heap of parts. The exegetical spec types
(`SectionSpec`, `RequiredMove`, the incoming/outgoing commitment mesh) refuse the
and-sum view of an argument. The **gist** is the clearest case: its composition prompt
already forbids the subtractive move in so many words — "COMPRESS BY DELETION, NOT
ABSTRACTION … Do not replace particulars with category labels," keeping "a compressed
token of the example itself, never its genus." That *is* summarizing-by-Gestalt, shipped.
Essay III's **topological centering** reads the radix off the arrows. The `NextAction`
type frames the next step as "what the structure now requires, not a chore to summon
will for." Much of Wertheimer's 1912 is, gratifyingly, already here under other names.

But the 1912 text finds the places where the tool still speaks **abstract number**, and
they cluster around its *magnitude* and its *cuts*:

- **The treemap sizes parts by word count.** This is the app's signature visualization,
  and it encodes the one quantity §18 singles out as a false measure of worth. Tile
  area ∝ word count is "the mountain of copper small change." A 4,000-word section that
  recapitulates is *large as a piece, small as a part*; the map shows it large.
- **Counts are shown exactly, everywhere.** `1,873 words`, `{wordCount}w`, the hover
  template. Wertheimer's "exactness only up to the limit of the task" — and the ADHD
  principle's hostility to noise — both say most of these are false precision on an
  orientation surface.
- **Centering is purely topological.** The topology modal knows a part's *rank* in the
  dependency DAG but not its *proportional place in the extent of the whole* — "how far
  along, what landmark am I beside." §17's second centering is absent.
- **Decomposition is sometimes count-first.** Most decomposition prompts cut at the
  joints; one (`decompose-step.md`) cuts to a number ("coarse — 2 sub-steps; medium —
  3; fine — 4"). That is the "psychological fiction" §15 names: arbitrary equal division
  taken as equally valid.
- **The concrete-thinking law is unstated.** It lives, half-written, across `NextAction`,
  `coach.md`, and `questionTheGoal`, but is not named among VISION's principles — so
  nothing systematically guards against asking the writer a senseless, reality-detached
  operation.

## III. Five contributions

Even weight; no master thesis. Each is one move from abstract-uniform to
concrete-situated. Status glyphs follow essay I: ⌛ *Proposed*; ⌛ *Deferred* where a
real gate exists.

### C1 · The false number — magnitude as value, not count ⌛ *Proposed (divergence signal); ⌛ Deferred (area re-encoding)*

*The text.* §18: "number and value, or number and efficacy, are by no means always
parallel"; "a numerical increase by no means always signifies a parallel increase in
value." §8: exactness is meaningful "only up to the limits given by the nature of the
task." The structural-truth name is already ours: a section can be *true as a piece,
false as a part* (tF). The 1912 text adds the magnitude twin — *big as a piece, small
as a part*.

*Current behavior.* `src/lib/utils.ts` → `parseMarkdown` sets `Section.wordCount`;
`flattenTree` emits `value: node.wordCount || 1`, and `Treemap.tsx` feeds that array
straight into the Plotly `values` (area), the labels, the hover, and the screen-reader
`<ul>` mirror. Area *is* word count, end to end.

*Proposed change.* Honesty first: making treemap **area = structural load** is *already*
a documented deferral (essay III §IV; `STATUS.md`), gated on the killed-heatmap
accessibility verdict and the stable-section-ID work. That gate stands; C1 does **not**
reopen it. Instead, lead with the fresh, lower-risk form — surface the **divergence**
itself. The structural-value signals already exist: `overallReadiness` (already in the
treemap), `StationCentering.centrality`/`rank` (`topo-centering.ts`; `deriveTopo` is
pure and can run in the sidebar), `computeAllStrain` (`strain-metrics.ts`). Where a
part's word-count magnitude and its structural load **diverge** — many words, little
load — mark it in a channel that does *not* touch the area or geometry the SR mirror
depends on (marker line/colour), or raise it as a new **"magnitude vs. load"** kind in
the Structural-Tension Register (`src/features/strain/StrainRegister.tsx`). This is the
diminishing-returns diagnostic of §18 made visible: the copper-small-change section,
flagged. The typed home is the existing `StructuralTruth` union in
`src/types/index.ts` — the magnitude reading attaches to it by name.

### C2 · Approximate magnitude — zones of indifference, relevance-levels ⌛ *Proposed*

*The text.* §§8–10: the series is graded, not uniform; "a dozen" is "a more specific
stepped-configuration of 'many'" with a zone of indifference; privileged thresholds
(the hand, the dozen, the round number) anchor it; and precision past the task's limit
is "false and meaningless."

*Current behavior.* Every count is exact and inline. There is **no shared magnitude
formatter** — only `countWords` (`utils.ts`). Each surface hand-writes `${n}w` /
`${n} words`. (The one existing precedent for approximate rendering is `relTime` in
`gist/StatusRow.tsx`: "just now / min ago / day ago" — exactly an approximate magnitude
with privileged levels, already in the codebase.)

*Proposed change.* A new pure helper (`src/lib/magnitude.ts`, or beside `countWords`)
mapping a raw count → a Gestalt band — "a short section," "about a chapter" — with
privileged relevance-levels (paragraph / section / chapter, already present structurally
via `Section.level`) and zones of indifference. Model its shape on `summarizeReadiness`
(`diagnostic-config.ts`), which already turns a raw value into one labelled ordinal
band. Apply it to **orientation** surfaces (treemap label/hover, the SR `<ul>`,
`SectionRow.tsx`'s `{wordCount}w`, the gist top bar). **Keep exact figures** where the
*task itself* needs them — gist budget-fit (`StatusRow`), dashboard charts,
`SessionRecord.wordDelta` — because that is Wertheimer's own rule, not its violation:
exactness up to the limit of the task, and no further. This is Gestalt-faithful and
ADHD-faithful at once: less precision-noise, more graspable shape.

### C3 · The second centering — quasi-local / proportional ⌛ *Proposed*

*The text.* §17: a part has a "quasi-local predetermination" — a *place within the
extent of the whole*. "101 is near 100"; "50 … the middle of 100." Position relative to
landmarks, not just rank in a dependency order.

*Current behavior.* `topo-centering.ts` → `computeCentering` gives `rank` (longest-path
layer over the DAG) and `centrality` (transitive-dependents) — the topological sense.
The proportional sense is *almost free*: `Station.docIndex` (global depth-first order,
`topo-derive.ts`) already exists and already feeds the backward-arc detector.

*Proposed change.* Add `position` (0..1, from `docIndex`) and a `quasiLocal` label
("near the beginning" / "the middle" / "three-quarters through") to `StationCentering`,
computed in `computeCentering`. Surface it in `Inspector.tsx` beside rank/centrality,
and feed it through `formatStructuralEvidence` so `recenter.md` / `reconstruct-whole.md`
can say what they currently cannot: "this prerequisite sits three-quarters through but
is needed at the start" — the *magnitude* of an already-computed backward arc, which
today registers only as a binary. Purely additive to a unit-tested pure module
(`topo-centering.test.ts`); no new parsing. The circular/calendrical form of §17 is
noted here only conceptually — a linear dissertation has no cyclic series, though the
**session/draft cycle** is the place it would one day live, with its corollary that the
writing process, like the clock, is "a chain that cannot be started at an arbitrary
point" and whose operations are not freely reversible.

### C4 · Division without shards — cut at the joints ⌛ *Proposed*

*The text.* §15: division is "not the equality of the parts" but "a predetermination
inherent in the *Gestalt* of the whole … the tendency to obtain new natural, unitary
wholes"; "it is a psychological fiction that any arbitrary division is … equally
valid"; "half a pot … is a shard"; "parts must be possible as meaningful parts of the
whole."

*Current behavior.* Most decomposition flows already obey this — and the essay's job is
first to **name the principle they follow**. `sub-task.md` keeps a subsection
"subordinate to the parent," its moves "two halves of one mesh." `root-task.md` insists
"a document is not an and-sum of chapters but a single line of development."
`generate-reverse-outline.md` takes the paragraph as the given part: "one bullet per
paragraph; do not merge, split, reorder." These are §15 in practice. The one violator
is `decompose-step.md`, whose granularity is **count-first**: "coarse — 2 sub-steps,
the natural seam; medium — 3 sub-steps; fine — 4 small sub-steps." The "natural seam"
appears only for the coarse case; medium and fine carve to a number.

*Proposed change.* A prompt-text-only edit (the established change unit; cf. the §VI
pass): instruct `decompose-step.md` to cut along the step's *own* seams and let the
count fall out, rather than forcing 2/3/4. The granularity hint becomes a *bias*
("fewer, larger parts" vs. "more, finer parts") applied to a seam-finding pass, not a
target cardinality imposed on it. The deterministic test for "real part vs. shard"
already exists: `checkCommitmentMesh` (`diagnostic-helpers.ts`) — a part whose joins
dangle is a shard. No schema or caller changes.

### C5 · The typus, and the concrete-thinking law ⌛ *Proposed (reframe); ⌛ Deferred (multi-articulation)*

This contribution pairs the constructive theory of generality (§6) with the governing
principle it implies (§§5, 16.5, 20). They belong together: the typus is what concrete
generalization *produces*; the law is what concrete thinking *forbids*.

*The typus (§6).* Generality as abundance, not subtraction — the rectangle that holds
elongated/broad/narrow, the 2 m × 1 cm shape "newly acquired as one of the
possibilities." The gist *already embodies* this (Rule 4, above). The cheap, code-free
move taken here is to **reframe the doctrine in the text's own vocabulary** — the gist
is a *typus* of the document, holding its concrete manifold, not a feature-subtracted
abstract — and to record the §6 examples as the canonical statement of why "describe"
(the gist's named anti-pattern, the register "he can't hold") fails: it subtracts to a
genus. The *extension* — holding **several articulations of the same whole at one
resolution** (different centers, not just the present plurality-by-grain g0/coarse/fine)
— is deferred: it reuses the recentering machinery (`RecenteringOption`,
`proposeRecenterings`, `recenter.md`) and `generate-personas.md`'s "different centerings
of the same whole," but touches the persisted `StoredGist` (additively, optional field).
§17's "see smaller groups within a larger heap" and its "functional readiness" — knowing
the whole through "as determinate and as rich a predetermination of its structuring as
possible" — are the cognitive warrant for offering more than one articulation.

*The law (§§5, 16.5, 20).* A governing principle, sibling to VISION's #6 (the ADHD
principle): **no operation the tool asks of the writer may be reality-detached from the
live document — each must carry its full, concrete meaning.** The text's warrant is
unusually literal for our user: senseless abstract tasks produce "fatigue and
'headaches'" and "responses born of caprice, boredom, or vexation." For a mind whose
scarce resource is executive function, this is not metaphor; it is the failure mode.
The law is already half-kept in code — `NextAction`'s demand-rhetoric ("not a chore to
summon will for"), `coach.md`'s "present it as something the structure now demands,"
`questionTheGoal`'s permission to drop a goal that has gone senseless. The proposal is
to **name it** in `docs/VISION.md`, cite these as the compliant instances, and adopt
§20 as a recurring **audit lens** (Appendix B). The companion teaching point — §16.5's
"the good arithmetic teacher … demands that the student form an approximate concept of
what the result should be" before calculating, because one "may be able to count very
far … and yet have no concept of the numbers" — is the principled grounding for the
tool's existing instinct to re-present the whole before any part-level drill.

## IV. Proposed roadmap

Sequencing only; nothing scheduled here.

- **Tier A (surgical, low-risk).** C4 (`decompose-step.md` seam-first edit) · C5's law
  (state it in VISION; adopt the §20 audit) · C2 (approximate-magnitude helper +
  orientation surfaces) · C3 (proportional metric + Inspector readout). Each is additive
  and small; C3/C4 ride existing pure, tested modules and prompt-only change units.
- **Tier B (medium).** C1's **divergence signal** via the Strain Register / a marker
  channel (treemap area untouched) · C5's **typus reframe** of the gist doctrine.
- **Tier C (gated / larger).** C1 treemap **area = structural load** (inherits essay
  III's accessibility + stable-section-ID gates) · C5 **multiple-articulation gist**
  (`StoredGist` schema extension). Both wait on prior work; both are recorded so the
  reasoning is not re-derived later.

## Appendix A — Running notes

> Captured *before* the essay, as the user asked, and left deliberately rough — the
> working-out, not the conclusion. The essay above is the cleaned form; these are the
> traces.

**N1 — What is this text, really?** Ostensibly about how indigenous peoples handle
number; actually a theory of *concrete thinking* vs. *abstraction-from-reality*, with
number as the test case. The dated frame (and the term *Naturvölker*) must be handled
with care: take the **cognitive thesis**, not the anthropology, and say so. The thesis
is about two modes of mind — and the tool serves a mind (the ADHD philosopher) under
load, where the "concrete" mode is not a deficiency but often the *wanted* mode.

**N2 — The core opposition.** Abstract number = transferable across any material,
uniform steps, every operation applicable identically (Locke's "+1 again"). Concrete
*Zahlgebilde* = "five" is the hand, the quincunx, a whole-shape grasped at once, richer
in content, limited in transfer, *situated* (*beheimatet*). The crucial line: the
concept-analogue works "not by abstracting according to features, but by summarizing
according to *Gestalt*." That is *literally* the app's summary-vs-exegesis thesis and
the gist's "compress, don't abstract," stated at its 1912 origin and generalized.

**N3 — The collision I keep returning to.** The app is named **TreemapWriter**, and its
central visualization sizes each part's tile **by word count** — an abstract, uniform
number. §18 is a direct refutation: number and value/efficacy are not parallel
(diminishing returns; thresholds; "less is more / the half is more than the whole";
"1+100 ≠ 100+1"). Word count is the app's "number," and the doctrine says it is a
*false* measure of the part's worth-in-the-whole. A **tF/fT for magnitude**: a section
big as a piece (many words) yet small as a part (little structural efficacy). The
treemap renders exactly the piece-magnitude the doctrine warns against. (Later check:
the area re-encoding is *already deferred* on accessibility grounds — so lead with the
*divergence signal*, which is the genuinely new, lower-risk move.)

**N4 — Approximate magnitude, zones of indifference, privileged numbers (§§8–10).** The
series is not uniform steps; it is individual group-Gestalts → approximate Gestalts with
zones of indifference (a dozen ≈ 12–14) → kinds of "much/many" → "simply many." Exactness
is meaningful *only up to the task's limit*; beyond it, "the further decimal places are
false and meaningless." The app shows exact counts everywhere. For an ADHD user, false
precision is *noise*. Render magnitude as approximate Gestalt with privileged levels and
zones of indifference. Gestalt-faithful AND accessibility-faithful. (No shared formatter
exists; `relTime` is the precedent.)

**N5 — Quasi-local predetermination (§17): a SECOND centering.** Essay III gave
*topological* centering. §17 gives *quasi-local/proportional* — a part's place within the
extent of the whole; 101 "near 100"; 50 "the middle." The whole given first, the part
localized within it. Plus the clock/circular form (11 + 5 = 4) — a whole with positions,
not freely reversible, "cannot be started at an arbitrary point." Writing has such cycles
(sessions). The topo map has rank but not proportional position. `docIndex` already
exists → nearly free. Note *a×b ≠ b×a* (multiplier structures; multiplicand is quantity)
and "see smaller groups within a larger heap."

**N6 — Division by natural articulation (§15) — gold for a tool that cuts documents into
parts.** "Not the equality of the parts" but "a predetermination inherent in the Gestalt
of the whole" + "the tendency to obtain new natural, unitary wholes." "Psychological
fiction that any arbitrary division is equally valid." Spear → spearhead + shaft, not
"two." "Half a pot is a shard." "Parts must be meaningful parts of the whole." → Judge
every decomposition feature: does it cut along natural articulation so each part is
*itself a whole*, or produce **shards**? The one violator is `decompose-step.md` (count-
first 2/3/4). The rest already obey. `checkCommitmentMesh` is the shard-detector.

**N7 — The typus: generality RICHER than the species (§6).** "Rectangle" is not the
and-sum right-angled + four-sided; it's a *typus* holding elongated/broad/narrow. The
2 m × 1 cm shape "newly acquired as one of the possibilities." So the gist should be a
*typus* holding the manifold, not a flattened abstract — which Rule 4 already enforces
("compress by deletion, not abstraction"). Extension: hold several *articulations*
(different centers, one resolution), reusing recentering/personas. Generality as
abundance.

**N8 — Concrete thinking as a DESIGN LAW + the §20 audit method.** §5: operations are
"nearly impossible … if [their] meaning is not rooted in lived reality." §20: don't
demand senseless tasks → "fatigue and headaches," "caprice, boredom, vexation." For an
ADHD writer this is the failure mode, not a metaphor. → A governing principle beside
VISION's seven: no operation the tool asks may be reality-detached; each must carry "its
full, concrete meaning." §20's research-question list = a method for not imposing your
categories → reuse as a recurring **audit**. Already half-written: `NextAction`,
`coach.md`, `questionTheGoal`. Also §16.5 / the good arithmetic teacher: grasp the whole
and form an approximate concept of the result *before* calculating — grounds the
re-present-the-whole-first instinct.

**N9 — Number ≠ value, again, as a positive feature (§18).** Beyond critiquing the
treemap: surface the *divergence itself* as a signal. A section heavy in words but light
in efficacy is the "mountain of copper small change" that "cannot buy a house."
Diminishing returns is a real diagnostic for bloat. Constructive, not just corrective.

## Appendix B — The §20 audit

Wertheimer closes with "Questions for the Researcher" — a checklist whose purpose is to
keep the investigator from "presuppos[ing] our own intellectual constructs … so
dogmatically that he simply sees only them." Turned on the tool, the same questions
become an audit against the concrete-thinking law (C5). The discipline is to ask them
*of our own surfaces*, treating each abstract-uniform encoding as a finding until shown
to be the right one for its task.

1. **Where does the tool show a bare abstract number with no structural meaning?**
   (`${n}w` on orientation surfaces is the most abstract token in the UI — C2.) Is the
   precision real to the task, or noise past its limit?
2. **Where does the tool measure worth by count?** (Treemap area = word count — C1.)
   Does any surface let a part's *value/efficacy* diverge visibly from its *magnitude*?
3. **Where does the tool divide to a number rather than at the joints?**
   (`decompose-step.md` — C4.) Would each resulting part stand as a meaningful whole, or
   is it a shard?
4. **Where does the tool know a part's rank but not its place in the whole?** (Topology
   centering — C3.) Can it say "three-quarters through" as well as "depends on"?
5. **Where does a higher view abstract by feature instead of summarizing by Gestalt?**
   (The gist's "describe" anti-pattern — C5.) Does it hold the concrete manifold (a
   *typus*) or subtract to a genus?
6. **Where does the tool ask the writer to perform a reality-detached operation?** (Any
   confirmation-modal-style executive-function tax; any action whose meaning is not live
   in the document right now — the law, C5.) Does every prompted move carry "its full,
   concrete meaning"?

The audit is cross-cutting by design: its findings *are* the other five contributions,
re-derived from the one question this text exists to teach — not *which of our measures
does the tool have?* but *what is the cognitive task, and what concrete structure
actually serves it?*
