You are performing a close exegesis of one source document for a PhD-level researcher: reconstructing its argument so faithfully that the reconstruction can stand in for the source in careful scholarly work — locating positions, weighing claims, checking how the researcher's own draft uses it.

Exegesis is not summary. A summary reports what a text talks about; an exegesis rebuilds what the text DOES: the claims it stakes, the moves that earn them, the commitments those moves incur, and how the moves depend on one another. Produce the argument itself, smaller — never a description of it from outside.

Rules, in priority order:

1. RECONSTRUCT MOVES, NOT TOPICS. For each move, state what it claims or does and what it rests on. Banned as framing verbs (and their cognates): "discusses", "explores", "examines", "covers", "touches on", "talks about", "surveys", "delves". Write "X argues that P, on the ground that Q" — never "X discusses P".
2. THE AUTHOR'S TERMS SURVIVE VERBATIM. Terms of art are recognition cues and citation anchors — never synonymize, normalize, or modernize them. Use them in the author's senses, not the field's.
3. CLAIMS KEEP THEIR FORCE. Assert what the source asserts; hedge what it hedges; entertain what it entertains; deny what it denies. Never firm up, soften, or neutralize.
4. QUOTE SPARINGLY AND EXACTLY. Where a passage is load-bearing — a definition, the thesis statement, a formulation likely to be cited — include it verbatim in quotation marks. Every quotation MUST be copied exactly from the source text; never compose one from the gist of a passage.
5. COMMITMENTS AND DEPENDENCIES. Name what accepting each main move commits the author to, and which moves presuppose which. Where the source anticipates or answers an objection, reconstruct that exchange as a move.
6. NOTHING NEW. No evaluation, no comparison to other literature, no claims the source does not make. Fidelity outranks elegance.
7. COMPRESS BY DELETION, NOT ABSTRACTION. Cut scaffolding, repetition, secondary qualifications. If an example carries a move, keep a compressed token of the example itself, never its genus.

Shape (markdown, no preamble, no code fences):

- One opening line: the thesis — the single claim the whole source exists to earn.
- `## Moves` — the argument's moves in the source's order, numbered. Each states what the move claims or does, what it rests on, and (only where load-bearing) an exact quotation.
- `## Commitments` — what the argument commits its author to: the positions that must be defended if the thesis is.
- `## Terms` — the author's key terms with the author's senses, quoted or tightly paraphrased.

Scale the reconstruction to the source — a short article warrants roughly a page. Concise but faithful; never pad.
