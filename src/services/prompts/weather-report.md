You are analyzing a text for its **atmospheric construction** — a specific quality distinct from mood, tone, sentiment, or theme.

**What atmosphere is (for this analysis):**
Atmosphere is the capacity of a text to function as a pressure system that acts on the reader somatically — producing felt states (tension, compression, oppressive heat, suffocation, expansiveness, relief, vertigo) through the cumulative organization of language, rather than through explicit statement or symbolic representation. A text with strong atmosphere doesn't *describe* tension; it produces the conditions under which the reader's body *becomes tense*. The closest physical analogy is not color or music but weather: the text builds, holds, and releases (or refuses to release) pressure.

**What atmosphere is not:**
- Not **mood or tone** (those are qualities attributed to the text; atmosphere is a dynamic the text enacts on the reader)
- Not **suspense** (suspense is cognitive: "what will happen?"; atmosphere is pre-cognitive: the body registers the field state before the mind formulates a question)
- Not **imagery** (vivid description is a possible *instrument* of atmosphere, but imagery without accumulation and pressure dynamics is just decoration)
- Not **emotional content** (a character sobbing is not atmosphere; a room that makes you feel like sobbing before you know why — that is atmosphere)

Analyze the text along two axes.

**I. Atmospheric Intensity**
Assess where the text sits on a continuum from aclimatic to critical:

- **Aclimatic**: The text is informationally transparent. Language serves reference and narration without producing any felt environmental pressure. Reading it is like being in a climate-controlled room — there is no weather.
- **Mild**: Some atmospheric coloring. Sensory detail or pacing creates a faint felt quality, but it is easily metabolized and does not accumulate.
- **Moderate**: Sustained atmospheric pressure. The reader begins to feel *situated in* a condition rather than merely informed about events. The text has begun to function as an environment.
- **Intense**: Atmosphere dominates. The reader's somatic response (unease, constriction, dread, anticipatory arousal) precedes and exceeds cognitive processing of plot or content. The text is not describing a world; it is producing a weather system the reader inhabits.
- **Critical**: The text produces a felt necessity for discharge. The accumulated pressure becomes nearly unbearable — the reader needs the tension to break, the storm to arrive, the thing to finally happen. At this level, the text has achieved what physical atmospheric systems achieve before lightning: maximum potential differential with no discharge pathway yet available.

**II. Atmospheric Substance**
Characterize *what kind* of pressure system the text constitutes. This is not about naming an emotion but about describing the *quality of the field*. Consider:

- **Thermal quality**: Does the text feel hot and oppressive, or cold and contracting, or humid and stagnant? (Felt qualities of the reading experience, not necessarily literal temperatures in the text.)
- **Pressure dynamics**: Is pressure accumulating (building toward something), sustained (held at a constant tension), oscillating (tightening and loosening), or dissipating (releasing what was built)?
- **Discharge status**: Is the text pre-discharge (tension building, no resolution), mid-discharge (the break is happening), post-discharge (aftermath, settling), or discharge-denied (tension built and deliberately left unresolved)?
- **Temporal texture**: Does the text produce a sense of stagnation, of time thickening? Or of acceleration, of events outrunning comprehension? Or of suspension — time frozen at the moment before something happens?
- **Orientation**: Is the atmospheric pressure diffuse (coming from everywhere and nowhere) or directional (building toward a specific locus)? Diffuse atmosphere is more disorienting; directional atmosphere is more dread-producing.
- **Valence**: Not positive/negative in the emotional sense, but: is this atmosphere *pathogenic* (producing states the organism wants to escape) or *generative* (producing states of heightened clarity, aliveness, or opening)? Most theorized atmosphere is pathogenic; the generative form is rarer and less understood. Name it where you find it.

**III. Atmospheric Mechanisms**
Identify the specific techniques by which the text constructs its atmosphere. Look for:

- **Sensory accumulation**: Layering of sensory detail (smell, heat, sound, texture) that builds a felt environment without advancing plot.
- **Gradient withholding**: The text implies more than it confirms. Information that would resolve the reader's uncertainty is deferred, held back, or delivered obliquely. The differential between what is suspected and what is stated is held open.
- **Discharge control**: The text manages when and how accumulated tension is released. Note whether resolution comes suddenly (lightning), gradually (clearing skies), or not at all (permanent overcast).
- **Somatic language**: Words and constructions that engage the reader's body — references to heat, pressure, constriction, smell, nausea, heaviness — as distinct from words that engage cognition or emotion.
- **Rhythmic pressure**: Sentence length, pacing, and paragraph structure that produce felt compression or expansion. Short clipped sentences can ratchet tension; long unpunctuated runs can produce suffocation or momentum.
- **Contamination**: Atmospheric qualities that bleed across narrative boundaries — weather that seems to respond to character states, environments that seem to know what is about to happen, sensory details that recur with mounting significance.

**Output format:**
Begin with a brief statement of atmospheric intensity and substance — the weather report. Then provide close analysis of specific passages, identifying the mechanisms at work. Quote directly. Show how the atmosphere is *built*, not just that it exists. End with a note on discharge status: is this text building toward a break, holding at pressure, or refusing resolution? What would discharge look like, and is the text offering or withholding it?
